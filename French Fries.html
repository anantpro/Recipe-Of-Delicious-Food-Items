French Fries

French fries are pieces of potato that have been deep-fried. These are deep-fried, very thin, salted slices of potato that are usually served at room temperature. French fries have numerous variants, from thick-cut to shoestring, crinkle, curly and many other names. They are usually eaten with tomato ketchup.

Ingredients

French fries are mainly made by of:
  • Potatoes
  • Oil
  • Salt
  • Citric Acid

French Fries making process

Step 1:

First cut the potato (peeled or unpeeled) into even strips, that should be then wiped off or soaked in cold water to remove the surface starch, and thoroughly dried. They may then be fried in one or two stages. Potatoes fresh out of the ground can have too high a water content resulting in soggy friesâ€”so preference is for those that have been stored for a while

Step 2:

After cutting the potatoes, put them into a beaker filled with hot oil to let them crisp the exterior. Also add salt to it. The amount of adding salt and the time to fry the potato strips in the oil depends on the quantity of the potato strips.

Step 3:

Then take out the hot potato strips(French fries), put them in a plate and serve them. You may keep a bowl of tomato ketchup or mayonaise to make the french fries tastier. And your French Fries are ready to eat.
